# Example — Lumen Candles · Brand Identity

*This is a DEMO brand so you can see what a finished output looks like. Delete the `workspaces/example-brand/` folder when you start your own.*

## Focus statement
**I help people who work from a small, noisy home carve out one calm corner — with a candle that signals "the day is done," not just "nice smell."**

- **Person:** renters and small-home dwellers, 25–40, who work where they live.
- **Tension:** "My home never feels like it switches off."
- **Path:** a ritual object, not a scent product — light it, the room changes, you exhale.

## Positioning
Most candles sell a fragrance. Lumen sells a **switch-off ritual** for people whose home and office are the same room.

## What we're against
- Candles marketed as luxury décor you're afraid to burn.
- 40-scent walls that turn a calm act into a decision.
- "Cozy vibes" with no actual point of view.

## Target audience
- **Primary:** WFH renters in small spaces; main frustration = "home never feels off-duty."
- Verbatim phrases: *"my brain won't clock out," "I need my space to feel different at night."*

## Voice
- **Always:** calm, plain, sensory, one idea per post.
- **Never:** hype, luxury-speak, scent-jargon soup, exclamation spam.
- In-voice: "You can't leave the office when the office is your kitchen table. So change the room instead."

## Messaging pillars
1. **Ritual over fragrance** — the act, not the note.
2. **Small-space calm** — designed for one corner, one room.
3. **Honest making** — hand-poured, small batches, real materials.

## Proof points & offer
- Primary CTA: start with the "one calm corner" set → lumencandles.example
- Hand-poured, small batch, 50-hr burn.
